## One-Line Story

ClinDiary uses voice-first local journaling, an encrypted document vault and on-device Gemma assistance to turn scattered health context into cautious summaries a person can review before speaking with a clinician.

## Reviewed Voiceover Draft

Before a medical appointment, the hardest part is often not a single symptom. It is remembering the full story clearly. What changed this week? How often did the pain happen? Did sleep, stress, medications, documents or wearable summaries explain part of the picture?

For many people, that context is scattered across memory, paper notes, phone reminders, medication boxes and devices.

ClinDiary is designed for this exact moment: helping people prepare clearer, safer conversations before care.

ClinDiary is a local-first mobile diary for daily health context. It is not an AI doctor. It is a private memory layer that helps users organize what happened between visits.

The user can start with a voice check-in. They speak in English, review the transcript and send it to Gemma. Instead of forcing every detail into a complex form, Gemma fills a check-in draft from what the user actually said: sleep, mood, stress, hydration, general pain, notes and other daily signals.

If symptoms are mentioned, Gemma can add recognized symptom drafts with severity from zero to ten, duration, body location and extra context. The user stays in control: unclear details can remain empty, Gemma can ask clarification questions and everything can be reviewed before saving.

The goal is not to diagnose the symptom. The goal is to preserve the details that are usually forgotten.

Medication context can be tracked too, including schedules, adherence context, notes and local reminders. But ClinDiary does not prescribe medications, suggest dosage changes or replace professional advice.

Important documents can be stored in an encrypted local vault on the device. This helps keep lab reports and medical documents close to the user, without turning them into ungrounded AI claims.

With Ask Files, the user can ask questions about saved demo documents. The answer is grounded in local document context and shows cited snippets, so the user can see where the information came from.

When the model is available, Gemma can help summarize and explain local context with cautious instructions and clear safety boundaries. The app exposes the active AI path, including provider, runtime, model and cloud-use proof, so users understand how summaries were generated.

Daily, weekly, monthly and pre-visit recaps help turn local entries into readable summaries before speaking with a clinician.

Safety logic stays conservative. Prevention reminders are rule-based prompts, not AI decisions about diagnosis or treatment.

The goal is simple: less forgotten context, more prepared conversations and privacy by default. ClinDiary is a memory layer for health, powered by local data and Gemma-assisted summaries.

## CapCut Assembly Notes

- Keep the first app recording visible by 0:12.
- Use captions for every voiceover line.
- Use a small persistent footer during AI scenes: "No diagnosis. No prescription. No emergency triage."
- Use gentle zooms on app screens, but keep UI text readable.
- Add blur masks for names, birth dates, notification previews, file names and medication labels if they are not fictional.
- Use app audio only if it does not distract; otherwise keep a quiet ambient track under the voiceover.
- Do not show hospital logos, real clinician names or real patient records.

## Required Captures Checklist

- Home screen with demo data.
- Daily check-in voice flow: Speak, transcript, Send to Gemma, filled metrics and symptom chips.
- Reviewed symptom save flow, or manual symptom entry if the voice capture does not produce clean demo symptoms.
- Medications screen with reminder context.
- Documents archive and upload entry points.
- Ask Files answer with sources.
- On-device model/status screen.
- Insights recap with on-device proof card.
- Prevention Center safety-oriented recommendation.
- Optional: Wearables and Health Dossier export if the capture is clean and does not push the video over 180 seconds.

## Lines To Avoid

- "ClinDiary diagnoses your condition."
- "ClinDiary tells you what medication to take."
- "ClinDiary performs emergency triage."
- "ClinDiary connects to hospitals and doctors automatically."
- "The Gemma model is bundled in every APK."
- "This demo uses real patient records."
- "Gemma always fills every field automatically."
- "The user does not need to review the generated symptom draft."
